📈 A Comparative Study of Mutual Fund Schemes :- 
An academic research project analyzing and comparing the performance of Equity and Debt mutual fund schemes across 5 major Indian Asset Management Companies (AMCs), using statistical risk-return metrics to guide investor decision-making.
Submitted to the University of Mumbai in partial fulfillment of the Bachelor of Management Studies (BMS) degree, Faculty of Commerce.

📌 Overview :- 
This project undertakes a risk-adjusted comparative analysis of mutual fund schemes offered by 5 leading Indian AMCs, evaluating them against standard market benchmarks (Nifty 50 / BSE Sensex) using established financial ratios.

The study is divided into two broad fund categories:
Equity Funds — Large Cap, Mid Cap, Small Cap, Multi Cap, and ELSS (tax-saving)
Debt Funds — Low Duration, Short Duration, Ultra-Short Duration, Medium-to-Long Duration, Money Market, Liquid, and Corporate Bond funds

🏦 AMCs Analyzed :-
#Asset Management Company:- 
 1) ICICI Prudential Mutual Fund 2) HDFC Mutual Fund 3) Aditya Birla Sun Life (ABSL) Mutual Fund 4) Nippon India Mutual Fund 5) SBI Mutual Fund

🎯 Objectives :- 
Analyze comparative performance based on NAV and historical returns (1-yr, 3-yr, 5-yr).
Evaluate risk-adjusted returns using Standard Deviation, Beta, and Sharpe Ratio.
Benchmark scheme performance against Nifty 50 / BSE Sensex to measure alpha generation.
Understand portfolio composition and asset allocation strategy across schemes.
Provide data-backed investment recommendations for retail investors.

🧮 Methodology & Tools :- 
Research Design: Descriptive research based on secondary data
Data Sources: NSE, BSE, Moneycontrol, ET Money, Morningstar, AMFI, and respective AMC websites
Study Period: 2021 – 2026 (5-year performance window)

Statistical Tools Used :- 
Standard Deviation (risk/volatility)
Beta (market sensitivity)
Alpha (excess return over benchmark)
Sharpe Ratio (risk-adjusted return)

💡 Conclusion :-
The study finds that mutual funds, when evaluated through proper risk-return analysis rather than headline returns alone, offer a diversified and professionally managed alternative to traditional investment avenues. Debt schemes suit low-risk-tolerance investors seeking stability, while equity schemes suit long-term, higher-risk-appetite investors seeking capital appreciation.
